About Me

My name is Hossein Zarezadeh. I hold an M.Sc. degree in Electrical Engineering (Control Systems). My professional focus lies at the intersection of control engineering, machine learning, and data engineering.

Background & Education

During my MSc I studied control theory, system identification, and simulation extensively, using MATLAB and Simulink for modeling dynamic systems. That foundation gave me a strong mathematical and analytical toolkit which I later applied to data-driven problems.

Machine Learning & Data

I build and evaluate models using Scikit-Learn for classical ML, as well as TensorFlow and PyTorch for deep learning tasks. I design pipelines for data ingestion, cleaning, and feature engineering using Pandas, NumPy, and Spark where appropriate. I am experienced with model validation, hyper-parameter tuning, and productionizing models.

MLOps & Data Engineering

I deploy and monitor models using Docker, MLflow, and CI/CD pipelines. For data engineering I have experience with SQL-based systems, ETL pipelines, and orchestration tools such as Apache Airflow. I am familiar with Kafka for streaming architectures and Spark for large-scale processing.

Simulation & Control

I apply control theory and simulation (MATLAB/Simulink) for system modeling and performance analysis. Combining simulation with data helps me design robust controllers and predictive maintenance solutions.

Goals

I aim to build reliable, scalable systems that combine domain knowledge from engineering with modern ML practices to solve industrial and research problems.
